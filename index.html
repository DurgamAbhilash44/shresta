Raw Cotton Bales

Our procurement process begins with the careful selection of raw cotton, followed by a meticulous cleaning process. The cleaned cotton undergoes advanced ginning procedures using state-of-the-art machinery, meticulously separating the valuable lint from the seeds. The resulting lint is then skillfully compressed into high-quality bales, each weighing 170 kgs. We recognize that proper packaging is paramount to maintaining the pristine quality of our product from production to delivery. To this end, we employ top-quality materials for tightly wrapping our bales, ensuring a seamless and secure transportation process. CONTAMINATION
Contamination, a major problem for Indian cotton, is the main reason for lower Indian cotton prices when compared to that of cotton from the rest of the world given the same quality parameters. At Shresta, we continuously try to reduce contamination levels throughout all stages of our production. Soon, Shresta Group plans to install Vetal scanner to reduce cotton contamination and provide the least contaminated cotton to our customers. How we are trying to handle this issue at our group:

STAGE 1 - Educating

Farmers not to use polypropylene / jute bags to transport cotton from field to factory. Labor to follow strict practices that will not add any additional waste to the cotton heaps.

STAGE 2- Examining

Physical and extensive examination of the cotton heaps by ground level labor to remove plastic threads manually.

STAGE 3- Deploying

We deploy specialized team and machinery to avoid seed cut fragments in the bales throughout the ginning process.

STAGE 4- Packaging

Apt Packaging and storing facilities to eliminate the interaction with all forms of contaminants.
